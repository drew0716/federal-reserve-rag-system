How much does it cost to produce currency and coin?
Currency
Each year, the Federal Reserve Board projects the likely demand for new currency and places an order with the Department of the Treasury's Bureau of Engraving and Printing, which produces U.S. currency and charges the Board for the cost of production. The 2025 currency operating budget is $1,040.0 million. The table below provides details on the variable printing costs of Federal Reserve notes for each denomination. The currency budget includes $283.4 million to reimburse for all variable printing costs. The variable costs represent the paper, ink, labor, and direct overhead costs to print each note.

The currency budget also includes $688.6 million to reimburse for fixed printing costs, which includes indirect manufacturing overhead and support, general and administrative, research and development, and prepress and engraving.
